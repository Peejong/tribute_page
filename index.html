Dr. Jose Rizal

June 19, 1861 – December 30, 1896

Autobiography

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was a Filipino nationalist and polymath during the tail end of the Spanish colonial period of the Philippines. He is tagged as the national hero (pambansang bayani) of the Filipino people. An ophthalmologist by profession, Rizal became a writer and a key member of the Filipino Propaganda Movement, which advocated political reforms for the colony under Spain.

He was executed by the Spanish colonial government for the crime of rebellion after the Philippine Revolution, inspired by his writings, broke out. Though he was not actively involved in its planning or conduct, he ultimately approved of his goal which eventually led to Philippine independence

He is widely considered one of the greatest heroes of the Philippines and has been recommended to be honored by an officially empaneled National Heroes Commitee. However, no law, executive order or proclamation has been enacted or issued officially proclaiming any Filipino historical figure as a national hero.
